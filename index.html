About Me

Hello there! My name is Tanima Sultana Nitu, and I am from the beautiful town of Panchagarh Sadar in Rangpur, Bangladesh. I come from a big family, and I believe that family is everything. It is where we belong, and it is what defines us.

Growing up, my family has always been my strength. We have not moved to an urban area, so we still live together as a wider circle with our extended family. My father is a shopkeeper, and my mother is a housewife. I have one sister who is a trainer for the Ministry of Women and Children Affairs, and I am the second daughter in my nuclear family.

I am proud to say that I have two aunts and four uncles, and my cousins are nineteen in number. Most of them are currently in school, except for the youngest ones. Having such a big family has taught me the value of teamwork and the importance of moral values.

I believe that my family has played a significant role in shaping who I am today. They have always been there to support me through the good times and the bad times, and I am grateful for their unwavering love and support.

In conclusion, I am just a simple girl from Panchagarh Sadar who believes in the power of family. I am proud of where I come from, and I will always cherish the memories and values that my family has instilled in me.
